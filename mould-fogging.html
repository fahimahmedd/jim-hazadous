Mould fogging is a highly effective treatment method that uses specialised equipment to transform liquid antimicrobial solutions into a fine mist, penetrating every crack, crevice, and porous surface that traditional cleaning methods cannot reach.

The process

A dry mist that reaches where cloths and brushes cannot.

Microscopic droplets (5-50 microns) remain suspended in the air for extended periods, settling on every surface – including walls, ceilings, and hidden cavities.

The result

Complete coverage. Complete elimination.

Complete coverage of all surfaces – including walls, ceilings, carpets, upholstery, and HVAC systems – eliminating mould spores at their source.

When to choose fogging

4 key applications
01

Widespread contamination

When mould affects large areas or multiple rooms, fogging provides uniform treatment throughout.

02

Hidden spaces

For mould inside wall cavities, ceiling voids, ductwork, and behind fixed cabinetry.

03

Porous materials

Carpets, upholstery, timber, and gyprock where wiping isn't possible or effective.

04

Post-remediation final treatment

As a final step to ensure all airborne spores are neutralised after physical removal.

The fogging process

Step 1: Preparation — The area is sealed and occupants, pets, and plants are removed. Sensitive items are covered or relocated. | Step 2: Fogging application — Specialised equipment generates microscopic droplets that remain suspended before settling.

Why fogging?

Complete coverage

Reaches every surface, including underneath and behind objects.

Non-invasive

No need to remove furniture or tear down walls.

Fast treatment

Most areas can be treated in 2-4 hours.

Long-lasting

Creates a residual barrier against future growth.

!

Important consideration

Fogging is highly effective for airborne spores and surface treatment, but it does not address the underlying moisture source. For lasting results, fogging should be combined with moisture control and prevention strategies.

Always pair fogging with source identification
